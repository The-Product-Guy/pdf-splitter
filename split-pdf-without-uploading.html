Privacy guide

Split PDF Without Uploading It

Use a browser-based PDF splitter when your document is private, sensitive, too large to upload, or simply should not leave your computer.

Open Private PDF Splitter Runs locally in your browser.

What "without uploading" means here

In the main PDF Splitter workflow, the file you select is read by your browser and processed locally with JavaScript. The PDF is not sent to our server for splitting. That means the document contents, filename, password, and page ranges are not needed by our server to create your output files.

Why local processing matters

PDFs often contain financial documents, contracts, identity paperwork, medical forms, academic files, or internal business material. A local browser workflow reduces exposure because there is no server-side upload step for the main tool.

What we still collect

We may use anonymous analytics to understand whether the tool is useful. Those events use broad categories such as split mode, file-size bucket, and completion status. We do not track PDF contents, filenames, passwords, exact file sizes, exact page counts, or custom ranges.

Best practice: For highly sensitive files, use a trusted browser, keep the site URL as https://smallpdfsplit.online, and avoid installing unknown PDF browser extensions.

FAQ

Can you see my PDF?

No. The main splitting workflow happens in your browser, not on our server.

Do I need to create an account?

No. The tool does not require login.

Does browser processing work offline?

The page must first load from the website. After the scripts are loaded, the selected PDF is processed locally in the browser.
